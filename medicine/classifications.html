Classification of Pharmaceutical & Herbal Treatments

Internal

Pills

  • Compressed solid topical drugs typically prepared in a small disc or sausage shape
  • To be ingested
  • Ingredients vary

Tonics

  • A drug that is drunk which is prepared for long periods
  • To be ingested orally
  • Ingredients vary

Tinctures for consumption

  • highly concentrated plant extracts usually using alcohol as extraction
  • To be ingested (usually added to food/beverages)
  • Typically alcohol, water and plant matter or extract

Brews

  • A drink made from steeped topical ingredients in hot or boiling water
  • Usuallu drank fresh or within a day
  • Water and topical ingredients

Syrups

  • A sugar solution containing plant extracts
  • Taken orally
  • Water, sugar, topical ingredients

External

Ointments/Unguents

  • Greasy almost paste like
  • Typically used for purposes that are antiseptic or geared towards heightened skin conditions (such as excessively dry skin)
  • Ingredients vary, beeswax to oil from 1:3 to 1:12
  • Topical ingredients

Salves

  • Solid - thinner consistency, soaks in quickly
  • For open wounds, dry/cracked skin
  • Beeswax to oil 1:4
  • Topical ingredients

Balms

  • Solid, stiffer consistency, needs to be rubbed/massaged in
  • Best for deep dermo-ailments, aches and pains
  • Beeswax to oil 1:1
  • Topical ingredients

Liniments

  • Oily substance with high alcohol content (which suspends oils of mint or pepper)
  • Applied externally, the oil stimulates mild irritation of the skin with the aim of lessening pain or imflammation in other areas of the body
  • Alcohol, camphor, oil (pepper or mint)
  • Topical ingredients

External Tinctures

  • Highly concentrated plant extracts usually using alcohol as extraction
  • To be applied to the affected area
  • Typically alcohol, water, plant extracts
