Agarita:

Berberis trifoliolata

Growth
The shrubs reach heights of 90 to 180 centimetres.

Leaves
Berberis trifoliolata is evergreen. The imparipinnate leaves are alternate. The elliptic leaflets are spiny and petiolate.

Flowers and Fruits
Berberis trifoliolata produces fragrant brilliant greenish yellow cup-shaped flowers in March and April. The plants are hermaphroditic.
The shrubs carry edible, ornamental berries.

Distribution
Berberis trifoliolata is native to Mexico.

Cultivation
Location and general conditions
The shrubs prefer a half-shady to shady situation on moist soil. The substrate should be sandy loam and comparatively rich. The plants tolerate temperatures down to -18Â°C.

Tolerance of special soil conditions
none: drought

Maintenance
Remove withered flowers if no fruit set is desired.
Lightly cut back all shoots after flowering.

Propagation
Propagate by sowing in autumn or by semi-ripe cuttings in early autumn.

External Uses

  • A tan-orange dye can be made from its wood.

Internal Uses

  • The leaves can be chewed for a natural anti-nausea.
  • The root can be used for an anti-viral and anti-diarrheal.
  • A tea can be made from the branches for help with digestion, constipation and as a blood tonic.
