Oregano:

Origanum Vulgare

Growth
The perennials have a clumpforming habit and reach heights of 30 to 90 centimetres. The plants usually grow to a width of 60 centimetres to 1 metre.

Leaves
Origanum vulgare is deciduous. The dark-green, simple leaves are opposite. They are ovate, entire and petiolate. The leaves have a softly pilose surface.

Flowers and Fruits
Origanum vulgare produces spikes of fragrant, vivid purplish red labiate flowers from July to September. The plants are hermaphroditic.
The perennials produce brown schizocarps in summer and autumn.

Root system
The plants form rhizomes.

Distribution
Origanum vulgare is native to all of Europe, Turkey, Cyprus, Iraq, Iran, West Sibiria, East Sibiria, Central Asia, Afghanistan, Pakistan, the Himalaya, Mongolia, Northwest Africa and China.

Cultivation
Location and general conditions
The perennials prefer a sunny situation on fresh to moist soil. The substrate should be comparatively poor with a pH between 8 and 10. The plants prefer gritty loam. The plants tolerate temperatures down to -18Â°C.

Maintenance
The plants need little to no maintenance if grown under suitable conditions.
Cut back faded flowering shoots in spring to promote compact growth.

Propagation
Propagate by sowing or by division.

Pests and diseases
Honeydew, galls and distorted leaves are a sign for an infestation with aphids. Use an insecticide or control biologically, e.g. with parasitic wasps or predators such as Aphidoletes aphidimyza.
Fine webs on the plants indicate an infestation with red spider mites. These sap-sucking insects mainly appear under glass and can be controlled either with insecticide or biologically with parasitic mites.
Sudden wilting and pale green discolouration indicate a fungal infection (phytophthora). Remove infected plants. Avoid by improving drainage and over-fertilization.
