Agave:

Agave americana

Growth
The perennials are comparatively slow-growing and reach heights of 1.8 to 2 metres. The plants usually grow to a width of 60 centimetres to 1 metre.

Leaves
Agave americana is evergreen. The grey-green, simple leaves are basal. They are lanceolate with spiny margins. The leaves have a glabrous surface.

Flowers and Fruits
Agave americana produces clusters of brilliant greenish yellow funnel-shaped flowers in July and August. The plants are hermaphroditic.
The perennials produce brown loculicidal capsules in autumn.

Distribution
Agave americana is native to Mexico and the Subtropics.

Cultivation
Location and general conditions
The perennials prefer a sunny situation on dry soil. They prefer sandy soil, gritty-sandy soil, sandy loam, gritty loam or sandy clay with a pH between 6 and 8. The plants need a soil depth of at least 76 centimetres for good growth. The plants tolerate temperatures down to -7Â°C.
Tolerance of special soil conditions
none: drought
low: calcareous soil

Maintenance
The plants usually require only a moderate amount of maintenance.

Pests and diseases
Cankers indicate an infection with fireblight. Generously remove affected parts and destroy them.
Waxy fibres and honeydew on leaves and shoots indicate an infestation with mealybugs. Apply insecticide or control biologically with predatory ladybirds.
Scale insects that sit on the undersides of the leaves and excrete honeydew can be controlled with insecticide or biologically with parasitic wasps.
