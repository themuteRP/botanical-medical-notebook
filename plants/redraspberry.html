Red Raspberry:

Rubus Idaeus

Growth The comparatively short-lived shrubs reach heights of 1 to 2.5 metres, they have a erect habit and form thickets. The main growing season is in spring. The plants usually grow to a width of 80 centimetres to 1 metre. Bark The bark is black and has a scaly surface. Leaves Rubus idaeus is deciduous. The middle-green, imparipinnate leaves are alternate. The ovate leaflets are biserrate and petiolate. The foliage is porous. The leaves have a tomentose surface. Flowers and Fruits Rubus idaeus produces racemes of white five-stellate flowers from May to August. The plants flower on last years shoots. They are hermaphroditic, pollination takes place by allogamy through animals. In summer the shrubs produce ornamental, edible red aggregate drupes that are persistent on the plant. Root system The plants form shallow roots. Distribution Rubus idaeus is native to all of Europe, Turkey, the Caucasus, West Sibiria, East Sibiria, Sakhalin, the Kamtschatka Peninsula, Central Asia, Mongolia, Manchuria, Korea, Japan, Alaska and Canada. Cultivation Location and general conditions The shrubs prefer a sunny situation on moderately moist soil. They prefer gritty loam with a pH between 5 and 7,5. The plants need a soil depth of at least 30 centimetres for good growth. The plants tolerate temperatures down to -40Â°C and need a frost-free period of at least 13 weeks. Tolerance of special soil conditions none: soil salinity high: drought Maintenance The plants need little to no maintenance if grown under suitable conditions. Propagation Propagate by sowing (the seeds require vernalization) or by ripe cuttings. Cultivars Autumn Bliss
