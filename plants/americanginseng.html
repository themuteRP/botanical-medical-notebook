American Ginseng:

Panax Quinquefolius

Growth The perennials reach heights of 20 to 50 centimetres. Leaves Panax quinquefolius has digitate leaves that are whorled. The obovate leaflets are serrate and petiolate. Flowers and Fruits Panax quinquefolius produces umbels of fragrant, brilliant yellow five-stellate flowers in July and August. The perennials carry ornamental red drupes. Distribution Panax quinquefolius is native to East Canada, the central Northeast of the US, the Northeast of the US, Oklahoma, the Southeast of the US and Florida. The plants are protected by CITES (the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora, also known as the Washington Convention). Cultivation Location and general conditions The perennials prefer a half-shady to shady situation on moist soil. They tolerate temperatures down to -18Â°C. Maintenance The plants need little to no maintenance if grown under suitable conditions.

Used for spiritual and ceremonial purposes.

Traditional uses included flu, colds, fever, sinus problems to reduce swelling and as a laxative.

The herb is smoked like tobacco and used in sweat baths. It is also dried for use in teas and tonics. It can be used as a body rub. Another not so common use is using the herb to attract a mate using Ginsing as a love charm.
