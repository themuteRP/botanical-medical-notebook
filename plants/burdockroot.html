Burdock Root:

Arctium Lappa

This plant is a member of the sunflower family and is also known as Cocklebur and Bardana.

Growth
The plants reach heights of 1 to 3 metres.

Leaves
Arctium lappa is deciduous. The dark-green, simple leaves are alternate. They are ovate, dentate and petiolate.

Flowers and Fruits
Arctium lappa produces corymbs of light purple many-stellate flowers in July and August.
The plants produce achenes.

Distribution
Arctium lappa is native to all of Europe, Turkey, the Caucasus, Iran, West Sibiria, Sakhalin, Central Asia, the Himalaya, China, Alaska, Canada, the Northeast of the US, the central Northeast of the US, the northern Prairie States of the US, the Southeast of the US, the Northwest of the US and California.
