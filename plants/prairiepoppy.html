Prairie Poppy:

Eschscholzia Californica

Growth
The comparatively short-lived plants have a erect habit and reach heights of 5 to 60 centimetres. The main growing season is from spring to fall. The plants usually grow to a width of 10 to 30 centimetres.

Leaves
Eschscholzia californica has middle-green, simple leaves that are alternate. The leaves are pinnatifid, lobate and petiolate. The leaves have a glabrous surface.

Flowers and Fruits
Eschscholzia californica produces cymes of showy, strong orange cruciform flowers from June to October.
The plants produce an abundance of brown fruits in summer.

Distribution
Eschscholzia californica is native to Oregon, California and Western Europe.

Cultivation
Location and general conditions
The plants prefer a sunny situation on dry to moderately moist soil. The substrate should be gritty-sandy and comparatively poor with a pH between 5,8 and 7,5. The plants need a soil depth of at least 25 centimetres for good growth. The plants tolerate temperatures down to -23Â°C and need a frost-free period of at least 17 weeks.
Tolerance of special soil conditions
none: soil salinity
medium: anaerobic soil, drought, calcareous soil

Maintenance
The plants need little to no maintenance if grown under suitable conditions.

Propagation
Propagate by sowing.

Pests and diseases
Leaf blotches are a sign of a fungal or bacterial infection. Bacterial spots are rather angular and yellow-rimmed while fungal spots usually are rather rounded with an area of fruiting bodies. Destroy affected parts, additionaly apply fungizide it is is a fungal infection.

Sudden wilting and pale green discolouration indicate a fungal infection (phytophthora). Remove infected plants. Avoid by improving drainage and over-fertilization.
