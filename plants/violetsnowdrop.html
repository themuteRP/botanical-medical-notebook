Violet Snowdrop:

mutation of Galanthus nivalis

Growth
The plants are comparatively fast-growing and reach heights of 7 to 20 centimetres.

Leaves
Galanthus nivalis is deciduous. The blue-green, simple leaves are basal. They are linear with entire margins and parallel venation. The leaves have a glabrous surface.

Flowers and Fruits
Galanthus nivalis produces solitary fragrant pendant, white cup-shaped flowers in February and March. The plants are hermaphroditic.
In spring the plants carry loculicidal capsules.

Distribution
Galanthus nivalis is native to Northern Europe.
