Blackberry:

Rubus Fruticosus

Growth The shrubs reach heights of 1 to 3 metres. Leaves Rubus fruticosus is deciduous. The dark-green, digitate leaves are alternate. The ovate leaflets are biserrate and petiolate. Flowers and Fruits Rubus fruticosus produces corymbs of white five-stellate flowers in June and July. Pollination takes place by allogamy through animals. The shrubs produce black aggregate drupes in autumn. Cultivation Location and general conditions The shrubs prefer a sunny situation on moderately moist soil. The substrate should be gritty loam.

External Uses

Internal Uses

  • A decoction of leaves is used as a gargle to treat sore throats, mouth ulcers, gum inflammations, thrush, and as a general mouthwash.
  • Making tea from the root is often used to soothe stomach problems, dysentery, and diarrhea has been found to be helpful in reducing swelling and stimulate the entire system.
