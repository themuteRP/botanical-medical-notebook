Allium Cepa

Growth
The plants are comparatively long-lived and reach heights of 30 to 100 centimetres. The plants usually grow to a width of 10 to 30 centimetres.

Leaves
Allium cepa is deciduous. The simple leaves are basal. They are linear with entire margins and parallel venation. The leaves have a glabrous surface.

Flowers and Fruits
Allium cepa produces umbels of white campanulate flowers from June to August. The plants are hermaphroditic.
The plants produce loculicidal capsules.
