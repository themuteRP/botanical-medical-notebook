Vanilla Flower:

Vanilla Planifolia

Growth The climbers reach heights of 30 to 1000 centimetres. Leaves Vanilla planifolia is evergreen. The simple leaves are alternate. They are lanceolate, entire and petiolate. Flowers and Fruits Vanilla planifolia produces racemes of light greenish yellow orchid-shaped flowers in July and August. The climbers carry edible and ornamental fruits. Distribution Vanilla planifolia is native to Central America, South America and the West Indies. The plants are protected by CITES (the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora, also known as the Washington Convention). Cultivation Location and general conditions The climbers prefer a sunny to half-shady situation on moist soil. The substrate should be sandy loam or sandy clay. The plants tolerate temperatures only above at least 1°C. Maintenance The plants can be considered rather high maintenance. Water moderately and mist regularly in summer, give little water and mist less in winter. Use soft water. Keep temperature at 20 to 25 °C in summer and at 15 to 17 °C in winter. Fertilization Apply orchid fertilizer monthly during growth. Propagation Propagate by tip cuttings in spring. Pests and diseases Damaged roots, withering, decolouration and dieback of leaves and shoots may be caused by stagnant moisture, fungi or bacteria. Remove affected plants. Prevent by improving hygiene and drainage.
