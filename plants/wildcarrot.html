Wild Carrot:

Daucus Carota

Growth
The plants reach heights of 80 to 120 centimetres.

Leaves
Daucus carota is deciduous. The dark-green, bipinnate leaves are alternate. The leaflets are linear and have entire margins.

Flowers and Fruits
Daucus carota produces compound umbels of white five-stellate flowers from June to September.
The plants produce achenes.

Cultivation
Location and general conditions
The plants prefer a sunny situation on dry soil.
