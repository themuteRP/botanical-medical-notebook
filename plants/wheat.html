Wheat:

Triticum Aestivum

Growth The grasses are comparatively fast-growing and reach heights of 60 to 100 centimetres. Leaves Triticum aestivum is deciduous. The middle-green, simple leaves are alternate. They are linear with entire margins and parallel venation. Flowers and Fruits Triticum aestivum produces racemes of brilliant greenish yellow flowers in June and July. The grasses produce ornamental brown caryopses in spring. Distribution Triticum aestivum is native to : garden origin. Cultivation Location and general conditions The grasses prefer a shady situation. They prefer soil with a pH between 5,5 and 8 and need a soil depth of at least 46 centimetres for good growth.
