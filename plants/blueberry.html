Blueberry:

Vaccinium Corymbosum

Growth
The shrubs reach heights of 1 to 5 metres, they have a erect habit and produce multiple stems. The main growing season is in spring and summer. The plants usually grow to a width of 1 to 1.5 metres.

Bark
The bark is reddish brown.

Leaves
Vaccinium corymbosum is deciduous. The dark-green, simple leaves are alternate. They are ovate with entire margins. The foliage is dense and turns an attractive yellow to red in autumn. The leaves have a glabrous surface.

Flowers and Fruits
Vaccinium corymbosum produces racemes of white pitcher-shaped flowers in May. The plants are hermaphroditic.
In summer the shrubs produce blue berries that are both edible and ornamental.

Distribution
Vaccinium corymbosum is native to East Canada, the central Northeast of the US, the Northeast of the US, the Southeast of the US and Florida.

Cultivation
Location and general conditions
The shrubs prefer a sunny to half-shady situation on fresh to moist soil. They prefer gritty-sandy soil with a pH between 4,7 and 7,5. The plants need a soil depth of at least 41 centimetres for good growth. The plants tolerate temperatures down to -29Â°C and need a frost-free period of at least 14 weeks.

The plants usually require only a moderate amount of maintenance.

Propagation
