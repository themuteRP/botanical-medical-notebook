Apple:

Malus Domestica

Growth
The shrubs are comparatively fast-growing and reach heights of 8 to 15 metres. The plants usually grow to a width of 7 to 10 metres.

Leaves
Malus domestica is deciduous. The grey-green, simple leaves are alternate. They are ovate, serrulate and petiolate. They turn an attractive yellow in autumn. The leaves have a glabrous surface.

Flowers and Fruits
Malus domestica produces corymbs of white five-stellate flowers in April and May. The plants are hermaphroditic, pollination takes place by allogamy through animals.
The shrubs produce green pomes in summer and autumn.

Pests and diseases
Leaf blotches are a sign of a fungal or bacterial infection. Bacterial spots are rather angular and yellow-rimmed while fungal spots usually are rather rounded with an area of fruiting bodies. Destroy affected parts, additionaly apply fungizide it is is a fungal infection.
