Alaskan Ginseng:

Oplopanax Horridus

Growth
The shrubs reach heights of 90 to 350 centimetres.

Leaves
Oplopanax horridus is deciduous. The middle-green, simple leaves are alternate. They are palmately lobed and petiolate.

Flowers and Fruits
Oplopanax horridus produces panicles of white five-stellate flowers in July and August.
The shrubs carry red drupes.

Distribution
Oplopanax horridus is native to Alaska, West Canada, the Northwest of the US, the Rocky Mountains, California, Michigan and Japan.

Cultivation
Location and general conditions
The shrubs prefer a sunny to shady situation on moist to wet soil. They tolerate temperatures down to -23Â°C.

External Uses

  • Can be applied to the skin to alleviate swollen glands, boils, boils, sores and skin infections.

Internal Uses

  • Made in to a tea it can help with wound recovery, fever, stomach trouble, coughs, colds and emptying the bowels.
  • May be chewed to cause vomiting.
