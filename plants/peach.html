Peach:

Prunus persica

Growth
The trees have a rounded to broadly spreading canopy and reach heights of 6 to 8 metres. The plants usually grow to a width of 70 centimetres to 1 metre.

Leaves
Prunus persica is deciduous. The dark-green, simple leaves are alternate. They are elliptic, serrate and petiolate.

Flowers and Fruits
Prunus persica produces solitary white five-stellate flowers in April. The plants flower on last years shoots. They are hermaphroditic, pollination takes place by allogamy through animals.
The trees produce yellow drupes in summer and autumn.

Distribution
Prunus persica is native to North China and Central China.

Cultivation
Location and general conditions
The trees prefer a sunny situation on fresh to moist soil. The substrate should be sandy loam, gritty loam or sandy clay with a pH between 6,5 and 7,5. The plants tolerate temperatures down to -23Â°C.
