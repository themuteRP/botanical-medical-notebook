Blood Flower:

Asclepias Curassavica

Growth
The subshrubs reach heights of 80 to 100 centimetres.

Leaves
Asclepias curassavica is evergreen. The middle-green, simple leaves are opposite. They are lanceolate, entire and petiolate.

Flowers and Fruits
Asclepias curassavica produces umbels of strong orange five-stellate flowers from June to September.
The subshrubs produce follicles.

Distribution
Asclepias curassavica is native to Florida, Mexico, Central America, Tropical South America and the West Indies.

Cultivation
Location and general conditions
The subshrubs prefer a sunny situation on moist soil. The substrate should be sandy loam or gritty loam. The plants tolerate temperatures only above at least 1Â°C.

Maintenance
The plants need little to no maintenance if grown under suitable conditions.
