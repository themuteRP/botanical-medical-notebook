Evergreen Huckleberry:

Vaccinium Ovatum

Growth
The shrubs reach heights of 1.5 to 2.5 metres and are comparatively slow-growing and long-lived. They have a erect habit and produce multiple stems, the main growing season is in spring and summer.

Leaves
Vaccinium ovatum is deciduous. The dark-green, simple leaves are alternate. They are ovate with serrulate margins. The foliage is porous.

Flowers and Fruits
Vaccinium ovatum produces racemes of light purplish pink tubular flowers in May and June. In summer the shrubs produce only few black berries that are both edible and ornamental.

Distribution
Vaccinium ovatum is native to British Columbia, the Northwest of the US and California.

Cultivation
Location and general conditions
The shrubs prefer a shady situation on fresh to moist soil. They prefer gritty-sandy soil with a pH between 5,5 and 7,5. The plants need a soil depth of at least 41 centimetres for good growth. The plants tolerate temperatures down to -18Â°C and need a frost-free period of at least 34 weeks.
